Myrmica

Sinopsis: Myrmica is a narrative video game of investigation and mystery. You will have to put yourself in the shoes of a US government agent and unravel the mystery hidden in an oil station that is trapped in a time loop. But don't trust anyone, nothing here is what it seems.

Released: 2024

Studio: Paranoia Studios

Plataform: PC

Skill Focus: Production & Game Design

Time Spent: 9 months

Engine and Tools: Unreal Engine 5, Notion, HacknPlan, Tom¬¥s Planner, Miro

Contributions

Producer

Myrmica was a project that lasted approximately one year, in which I worked as a producer and designer alongside a team of 35 people.

First, I was in charge of production from the beginning, during the initial brainstorming and concept development phases, as well as creating a production plan.

Later, the development phase began, in which I was responsible for coordinating the different departments as more colleagues joined the project. During this stage, a task tracking system was used with the hacknplan tool, and the deadlines established in the pre-production phase were followed using the Tom's Planner tool.

Finally, in the post-production phase, I collaborated with the quality control team to classify bugs during the beta phase, reducing blocking issues before the presentation.

Narrative Designer

As for my role as a designer, I was first part of the narrative department and was responsible for creating the world, establishing the story, and designing the different characters that star in the video game.

The main tasks were to create a futuristic and dystopian world where we could set our mystery story and time loops. As for the characters, our idea was to design different characters with very distinct characteristics, each of them unique. This way, every interaction between the protagonist and the other characters would lead to very interesting situations.


Game Design

Another of my tasks as a designer (outside the narrative department) was mission design. For this video game, we thought about designing a system of interconnected missions that contained a series of blocks to guide the player but give them freedom.

Our idea was to create a mission system that would offer the player initial freedom to perform different actions but force the player (through subtle obstacles) to complete missions in a specific order. This idea is reinforced by the integration of a time loop system that allows the player to repeat the day and continue with the mission that interests them most.
