Time Killer

Sinopsis: Time Killer is a 3D platformer where you will have to prove your skill by completing levels in a race against time. Can you become a real Time Killer?

Released: 2025

Studio: Independent

Plataform: PC

Skill Focus: Production & Game Design

Time Spent: 5 months

Engine and Tools: Unity, Notion, Trello, Jira

Contributions

Producer

The production of Time Killer had its easy and not-so-easy parts.

The complicated part of this project came in post-production. At this stage, we focused on testing the game countless times to adjust the difficulty of each level, adjust the pacing, and achieve the perfect game feel.

Once we had the final version of the game, we moved on to the polishing and bug-fixing stage. At this stage, we all had to work together to find and fix all the bugs and glitches that existed or could appear. It was a complicated task due to the countless bugs that appeared because of the different situations that could arise. Despite this, we managed to produce a polished and fully playable version.

Game Designer

My work as a generalist designer was based on different tasks:

Designing the main mechanics that the player could perform.

Creating different types of enemies (different abilities, types of shots, and special AI behaviors) and dynamic elements of the environment.

Adjusting the difficulty of each level and achieving a game feel appropriate for the player's mobility

Level Designer

In the design department, I focused on all the design work for the different levels that make up the game.

The first steps involved creating different test levels to adjust player metrics and variables in order to establish the exact values that would be used in the final game.

Later, we began designing the levels, sketching their layouts and creating prototypes with the ProBuilder tool (greyboxing).

Once the levels were complete, we iterated and integrated enemies and different elements to add dynamism to the game. This was a very interesting stage where I experimented with different mechanics and level design styles in order to offer a totally unique and special experience in each level.
